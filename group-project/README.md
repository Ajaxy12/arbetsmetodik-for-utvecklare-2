### ğŸ”„ Agile Methodologies

**Scrum** is a framework/methodology (not a tool) for managing complex work through iterative sprints.

#### Major Components:
- **Roles:** Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers
- **Events:** Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective
- **Artifacts:** Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment
- **Time-boxed sprints:** Typically 1-4 weeks with fixed start/end dates
- **Regular inspection and adaptation:** Review and adjust after each sprint

#### When to Use Scrum:

**For Groups:**
- âœ… When you have a team (3-9 people recommended)
- âœ… When you need structured planning and regular check-ins
- âœ… When work can be organized into fixed time periods (sprints)
- âœ… When you need clear roles and responsibilities
- âœ… When you want regular demos and feedback cycles
- âœ… For complex projects requiring coordination

**For Solo Work:**
- âœ… When you want structure and discipline in your workflow
- âœ… When you have deadlines and need time-boxed work periods
- âœ… When you want to practice Scrum methodology
- âœ… When you need regular self-reflection and planning
- âš ï¸ Less ideal for solo work (designed for teams)

---

**Kanban** is a visual workflow management method (not a tool) using a board with columns.

#### Major Components:
- **Visual board:** Columns like Backlog, Doing, Test, Done
- **Work items:** Move through columns as they progress
- **Continuous flow:** No fixed time periods (unlike sprints)
- **Work in Progress (WIP) limits:** Control how much work is active at once
- **Transparency:** See all work and identify bottlenecks easily
- **Flexibility:** Add/remove items anytime, no sprint boundaries

#### When to Use Kanban:

**For Groups:**
- âœ… When you need flexibility and continuous delivery
- âœ… When work items vary in size and duration
- âœ… When you want to visualize workflow and bottlenecks
- âœ… When you need to prioritize and reprioritize frequently
- âœ… For support/maintenance work with unpredictable tasks
- âœ… When team members work on different timelines

**For Solo Work:**
- âœ… Excellent for personal projects and task management
- âœ… When you want simple, visual organization
- âœ… When you have varying task sizes and priorities
- âœ… When you need flexibility without fixed deadlines
- âœ… For ongoing work without clear sprint boundaries
- âœ… Perfect for managing multiple projects simultaneously

---

#### Quick Comparison:

| Aspect | Scrum | Kanban |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Time Structure** | Fixed sprints (1-4 weeks) | Continuous flow |
| **Planning** | Sprint planning meetings | On-demand planning |
| **Best For Groups** | âœ… Structured teams | âœ… Flexible workflows |
| **Best For Solo** | âš ï¸ Can work but less ideal | âœ… Excellent fit |
| **Complexity** | More structured | Simpler, more flexible |
| **Commitments** | Sprint goals and commitments | No fixed commitments |
| **Meetings** | Regular ceremonies required | Minimal meetings needed |

**In This Project:** We're using a hybrid approach - Scrum's sprint structure with Kanban's visual board (markdown files) for flexibility.
